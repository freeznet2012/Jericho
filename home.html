1-Gandhi came from a poor family, and he had dropped out of the cheapest college he could afford. 2-Gandhi's uncle Tulsidas also tried to dissuade his nephew. 3-Gandhi wanted to go. 4-To persuade his wife and mother, Gandhi made a vow in front of his mother that he would abstain from meat, alcohol and women. 5-Gandhi's brother Laxmidas, who was already a lawyer, cheered Gandhi's London studies plan and offered to support him. 6-Putlibai gave Gandhi her permission and blessing.[45][49]. 7-Upon arrival, he stayed with the local Modh Bania community while waiting for the ship travel arrangements. 8-The head of the community knew Gandhi's father. 9-After learning Gandhi's plans, he and other elders warned Gandhi that England would tempt him to compromise his religion, and eat and drink in Western ways. 10-Gandhi informed them of his promise to his mother and her blessings. 11-The local chief disregarded it, and excommunicated him an outcast. 12-But Gandhi ignored this, and on 4 September, he sailed from Bombay to London. 13-His brother saw him off.[48][50]
